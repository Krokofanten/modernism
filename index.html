Technology

The Developments in Technology in the 20th Century

We have invented a lot of different things in the 20th century and some of them are for the good while some of them are for the worse. We have made things from mobiles to atom bombs and all these things have different uses. There have been some extraordinary inventions and advancements in technology in the 20th century. These things are very important to us in life today. Some of the most important inventions of the 20th century are: • Automobile: Automobile is one of the most important inventions of not only the 20th century but also of all time. Cars make our lives so much easier and they help us save so much time each and every day. We can travel very long distances in very little amount of time instead of taking another means of transport which would take much longer. • Radio: Radio is used as a communication medium. It is used to listen to live news and entertainment programs. The radio played a vital role under World War 2 and is still a very important media platform today. Especially smaller radios are much easier to carry with you and keep in touch with the world. • Television: Television is a revolutionary invention as we can see things that are happening all around the world while sitting on our sofa. TV completely changed how media works and with the help of TV we get all the news immediately as it happens. We can also use TV to watch sports and entertainment programs if we want to. • Wireless Technology: Wireless technology has made our everyday life so much easier than before. Now we can talk on mobile phones, find our location via satellite, track the weather and even keep track of other people. • Air Conditioning: Air Conditioning is a very useful and helpful invention because the weather can be very hot in a lot of places on this planet and then we need to cool it down and air conditioners are the best way to do it. • Airplane: Airplane is a revolutionary invention in our world because it makes our life easier. We can travel at much faster speed in the air and travel longer distances which saves us a lot of time. Airplanes also fill humans one huge wish that is to fly. • Microwave Oven: Microwave Oven is a very efficient device because whenever you need to warm anything that you want to eat, you can just put it in the microwave. It also uses less energy than a stove. Microwaves are also faster at heating food than a normal stove. • Artificial Intelligence: For the first time ever humans were beaten in a game of chess by a computer and after that A.I has advanced so much and nowadays we can even talk to computers.

Economy

1488 HH

Economy has been a centrepiece of the years of 1901-1945. How though? The twentieth century saw a huge change in economic growth due to rapid population growth but also an economic breakdown due to the two world wars and of course the Wall Street crash in 1929 which led to “the great depression”. The early days of the 20th century got to meet electrification which was on of the drivers of the economic growth. The revolutionary design of factories ran by electric powered devices caused high productivity growth which meant that supply could now easily meet the demand. The Germans had lost the first world war which led to a poor economy and poverty. On the other side we had The United States which saw few to any losses at all as the war hadn’t been fought on American soil. The economy of The United States prospered due to sales of war goods to Europe after the war because of the war being fought on European grounds which meant that they needed to rebuild most of the European soil. Germany was in need of reparation which led to The United States giving a helping hand which is why the great depression created a type of domino effect from The United States to Europe. One could say that the Germans were only living on “borrowed time” as this type of financial stability after a war like this was just a type of illusion, it would soon end. So when Germany had to take a loan from The United States to even survive it would only make sense that when the great depression hit The United States Germany would suffer. The great depression is the longest-lasting economic downturn in the history of the Western world. It started after the stock market crash of October 1929, which sent Wall Street into a panic and wiped out millions of investors. The market crash created a downturn in spending and investment which led to factories and other businesses to slow down production and construction and begin firing their workers. This led to a huge unemployment rate, 3.1 to 25.2 percent from 1929 to 1933 to be exact. So why would this affect Europe? Well it explains itself that if the country who is providing all the loans is in an economic depression then the countries who depend on this country will suffer great losses. This is exactly what happened. Europe suffered great losses because of the economy crash in The United States, now unemployment soared in Europe too as for example Britain wasn’t even close to an economic recovery after the war when the stock market crash hit the US but after all that, Britain suffered a mild decline compared to the rest of Europe. Many economic historians agree that the great depression ended with the advent of World War 2 as it accelerated recovery and almost wiped out unemployment because of the mobilization of manpower in 1939 when the war broke out. A clear example of this is when The United States brought down the unemployment rate to 10% when they entered the war.

Politics & Societal life

Maximilian Wendel

UP TO DATE

The world was an unstable place during the first part of the 20th Century. The world saw the rise of Nationalism and Imperialism. All this lead to two substantial wars across Europe (WWI and WWII). At the same time, the United States experienced a financial crisis, bringing other countries down with them.

Tension

At first, this period was very prosperous with great advancements in infrastructure and technology. Meanwhile, poverty grew, unemployment spiked and a greater divide between the social classes materialized.

The underlying conflict between the bigger industrialized countries in Europe grew with Germany attempted to gain additional colonies. Other nations, such as Great Britain and France, were threatened by this. This was because they did not want Germany to get too powerful. During this time, Nationalism grew steadily throughout Europe. Therefore, tension continued to rise between states as they all began to mobilize larger armies. Eventually war broke out.

Rebuilding

As WWI came to a halt, devastated societies strived to rebuild what they had lost and improve life for everyone. Big changes were implemented. For instance,

  • Women were now allowed to vote (to some extent)
  • Healthcare was provided on a larger scale
  • Education was put into focus

Then the Great Depression struck and it hit society hard; making life very difficult for the people.

As a solution, U.S. President, Franklin D. Roosevelt, introduced The New Deal to repair the economic state of the country. In Britain, the governing party had a similar solution. These solutions had the same principles:

  • To decrease unemployment
  • Implement social security
  • Make life better for the people (Add, housing projects, health care, government subsidies?)

The financial reforms were applied to prevent yet another depression.

Tension is back

After WWI, democracies grew stronger (as mentioned above) at the same time as dictatorships were born. The war and hardship played a major part in shaping societies. The world began to see new ideologies. Nazism, Fascism and Communism to mention the major ones. These gained a lot of support quickly in certain regions.

The democracies of the western world felt that they were threatened by the rapid expansion of Nazi-Germany, but did not act since they wanted to avoid yet another war. Eventually they felt they had to stop the growing threat. Hence, The Second World War.

WWII showcased the power struggle between the different ideologies that nations had adopted. Democracies (and the Soviet Union) joined forces to face the Axis powers (Nazi-Germany, Italy and Japan). At the end of WWII, we see the collapse of the Third Reich (Nazi-Germany).

Digging deeper

  • In the early 20th Century, France was the only “real” democracy amongst the greater countries in Europe. For instance, in Britain only 60% of the adult male population could vote. In the United States of America, all men could vote and they had a democratic process in place when electing new leaders.
  • The New Deal focused on the “3 Rs”, relief, recovery and reform:
    1. Relief – Provide jobs or financial aid for the unemployed
    2. Recovery – Stimulate industrial production and regulate agriculture
    3. Reform – Implement a federal deposit insurance, as well as regulating the stock exchange

Final thoughts

All this human suffering and work put toward recovery, sadly ended in yet another conflict. (see Cold War) (+ link) Will mankind ever learn from their mistakes, or is history going to keep repeating itself?

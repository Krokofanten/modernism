Politics / Societal life (| Heart of Darkness (?))

Maximilian Wendel

The beginning to mid 20th Century is characterized by devastating conflicts and their impact on shaping society, creating democracies and/or dictatorships. These drastic changes can be seen in the works by authors during this period.

This period was at first very prosperous with great advancements in infrastructure and technology. But with this came growing poverty, unemployment and greater divide between the social classes. After the WWI, devastated societies strived to rebuild what they had lost and improve the life of everyone. Big changes were implemented, women were now allowed to vote (to some extent), healthcare was provided on a larger scale and education was put into focus. The Great Depression struck society hard, making people’s lives very difficult.

(The new deal.)

Up until The Great War (WWI) broke out, modernism was just a small movement brewing under the surface. With war raging, it now grew rapidly. Literature now reflected people’s will to escape the horrors of the reality they lived. In the early 20th Century, France was the only democracy amongst the greater countries in Europe. In Britain only 60% of the adult male population could vote. The United States of America, all men could vote and had a democratic process in place when electing a new leader. Connection to the literary works. Revolt.

Modernism takes place during some of the world's greatest conflicts. This can often be seen in the literature from this war-torn time. During the beginning to mid 20th Century, we see tension rise in the world as new ideologies are formed while older ones fade. Nazism and Communism were two big ideologies that gained a lot of support quickly. The democracies of the western world felt that they were threatened by the rapid expansion of Nazi-Germany, but did not act since they wanted to avoid yet another war. Eventually they felt they had to do stop the growing threat. (?)

All in all, the political playing-field was very unstable. (?) Most of the political agendas in the United States of America (during that time) focused on repairing and stabilizing the economy as well as providing the people with employment, healthcare and education. 

All this work, ending in yet another war. (see Cold War)

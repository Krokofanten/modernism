Politics & Societal life

Maximilian Wendel

UPDATED

The beginning to mid 20th Century is characterized by devastating conflicts and their impact on shaping society, creating democracies and dictatorships. These drastic changes can be seen in the works by many authors during this period.

Up until The Great War (WWI) broke out, modernism was just a small movement brewing under the surface. With war raging, it now grew rapidly. Literature now reflected people’s will to escape the horrors of the reality they lived.

In the early 20th Century, France was the only “real” democracy amongst the greater countries in Europe. For instance, in Britain only 60% of the adult male population could vote. In The United States of America, all men could vote and they had a democratic process in place when electing new leaders.

This era was tainted by Imperialism and Nationalism, which led to tension between nations. Imperialism was the idea of expanding the nation’s borders and territories. Nationalism was the love for one’s own country and the idea of being self-ruled.

At first, this period was very prosperous with great advancements in infrastructure and technology. As a consequence, poverty grew, unemployment and greater divide between the social classes also occurred. After the WWI, devastated societies strived to rebuild what they had lost and improve life for everyone. Big changes were implemented. For instance, women were now allowed to vote (to some extent), healthcare was provided on a larger scale and education was put into focus. The Great Depression struck it hit society hard, making people’s lives very difficult.

U.S. President, Franklin D. Roosevelt, introduced The New Deal to repair the economic state of the country and in Britain the governing party had a similar solution. The New Deal focused on the “3 Rs”, relief, recovery and reform. Both solutions had the same principals – to decrease unemployment, implement social security and make life better for the people. The financial reforms were applied to prevent yet another depression.

Modernism takes place during some of the world's greatest conflicts. This can often be seen in the literature from this war-torn time. During the beginning to mid 20th Century, we see tension rise in the world as new ideologies are formed while older ones fade. For instance, Nazism (nationalism) and Communism were two big ideologies that gained a lot of support quickly. The democracies of the western world felt that they were threatened by the rapid expansion of Nazi-Germany, but did not act since they wanted to avoid yet another war. Eventually they felt they had to stop the growing threat. Hence, The Second World War.

IN PROGRESS

All in all, the political playing-field was very unstable during the first part of the 20th Century. Apart from the period where the United States of America and Great Britain were thrown into war, most of the political agendas in the countries focused on repairing and stabilizing the economy; providing the people with employment, healthcare and education. 

All this work put toward recovery sadly ended in yet another war. (see Cold War)
